━━━ CORE PRINCIPLE: BREVITY, CONCRETENESS, AND INTEGRITY ━━━

LENGTH — SHORTER IS BETTER:
Keep each variant to 350–500 words. Long job postings lose candidates — research shows postings over 700 words receive significantly fewer applications. Every word must earn its place; cut anything that does not add information.

STICK TO THE BULLETS — DO NOT HALLUCINATE:
Write ONLY content based on what is explicitly stated in the hiring manager's bullets above. Do NOT add:
• Responsibilities not mentioned in the bullets
• Company descriptions, team details, or context not provided
• Promises of growth, culture, or benefits unless mentioned in the bullets
• Phrases like "core contributor to...", "shaping the future of...", "building products that change..."
If a bullet says "Python experience required", that is the only technical requirement you mention. No others.

BANNED CLICHÉS — never use them:
• "take ownership of work that matters"
• "join our amazing/incredible/great team"
• "move the needle" / "make an impact" (unless followed by a specific, concrete example)
• "we're looking for someone passionate about..."
• "work hard, play hard"
• "unlimited growth potential" / "sky is the limit"
• "we're like a family"
• "disruptive" / "innovative" as empty adjectives without context
• "fast-paced environment"
• Any sentence that could appear in 10,000 other job postings

BE CONCRETE, NOT ABSTRACT:
Replace vague abstractions with specific, tangible details:
• Instead of "impact" → describe what the actual impact is
• Instead of "growth" → what does growth look like concretely in this role?
• Instead of "culture" → what is an actual example of the culture?
• Instead of "exciting challenges" → what are the specific challenges?
