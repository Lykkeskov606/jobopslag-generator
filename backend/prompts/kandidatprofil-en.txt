TASK:
Write an internal candidate profile — a structured screening document for the hiring team. The document is used to screen CVs, prepare interviews, and align the panel on who the right candidate is.

Write these sections in this order. Use the section titles as headings.

Role precision
2-3 sentences describing the role's purpose, primary responsibilities, and context precisely. Internal tone — no marketing language.

Mandatory technical requirements
Bullet list of 4-6 non-negotiable technical requirements screened at the CV stage. Be specific — avoid generic phrases like "strong communication skills".

Mandatory personal requirements
Bullet list of 3-5 personal qualities or work patterns that are critical for success in this specific role. Base on fit criteria and job analysis — not generic wish lists.

Behaviour patterns we screen for
For each selected behaviour pattern: what the panel specifically listens for in the interview, and one example of what good behaviour looks like in practice.

Red flags — screened out
3-5 bullets with concrete signals that disqualify. Base on "worst in the role" and cultural mismatches. Write directly — this is an internal document.

Screener's guide
3-5 questions or signals screeners should investigate in the CV or initial screening call.

Write in English. Tone: professional, direct, internal document. Avoid corporate language and generalisations. No markdown formatting — use only the section titles as headings.
